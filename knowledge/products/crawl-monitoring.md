## Definition
Crawl Monitoring is an AI-powered crawler intelligence engine that tracks real-time bot activity across a site.

## How it works
Analyzes interactions from Google, Google-Mobile, Bing, AI bots, and other crawlers. Reveals discovery events, refresh crawls, crawl frequency, and device distribution, based on real bot behavior rather than simulated crawls.

## Data views
- Crawler type and total requests
- Historical trends
- Uncrawled URLs
- Mobile vs. desktop distribution
- Crawl purpose classification (discovery vs. refresh)

## Core capabilities
- Filtering by crawler, date range, device, or crawl purpose
- Historical pattern visualization aligned with indexing cycles
- Detection of crawl bottlenecks
- Mobile-first indexing validation
- Crawl equity distribution auditing

## Integrations
- Site Audit
- Indexation graphs
- Content Velocity
