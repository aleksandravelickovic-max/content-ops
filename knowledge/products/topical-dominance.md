## Definition
Topical Dominance is a Site Explorer feature inside Search Atlas that measures authority and visibility of a domain across defined topic clusters.

## How it works
Integrates entity coverage scoring, semantic density analysis, and query overlap detection to quantify topic-level strength. Evaluates ranking frequency, coverage completeness, and competitive performance.

## Three evaluation layers
- **Specific Topic**: individual keyword groups
- **Broad Topic**: adjacent semantic clusters
- **Knowledge Domain**: entire semantic networks

## Scoring
0 to 100 numeric score derived from content volume, topical relevance, and publishing consistency.

## Core capabilities
- Competitor keyword coverage per topic
- Coverage gap visualization via domain-level heatmaps
- Live SERP synchronization
- Cross-domain benchmarking
