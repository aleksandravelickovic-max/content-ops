## Definition
SCHOLAR (Semantic Content Heuristics for Objective Language Assessment and Review) is a proprietary semantic evaluation system inside Search Atlas that measures factual precision, linguistic clarity, and information value in SEO content.

## How it works
Interprets how Google content quality systems rank text based on Page Quality (PQ) and Information Retrieval (IR) signals. Applies a semantic triple model (Entity, Attribute, Value) and Subject, Predicate, Object (SPO) logic to decompose statements into analyzable units.

## 12 evaluation dimensions
1. Overall Score
2. Content Clarity
3. Factuality
4. Human Effort
5. Information Gain
6. Content Freshness
7. User Intent
8. Entity Score
9. Contextual Flow
10. Numerical Content
11. Query Relevance
12. Readability

## Output
Composite SCHOLAR grade combining lexical (word-level) and contextual (concept-level) signals. Mirrors search system evaluation of helpfulness, topical authority, and semantic density.
