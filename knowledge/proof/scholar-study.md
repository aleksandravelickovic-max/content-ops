## Study
"Optimizing Scholarly Work for Search Engines: Evidence from the SCHOLAR Dataset", executed by Manick Bhan and the Search Atlas research team.

## Measured correlations
- Query relevance score: −0.179
- User intent alignment score: −0.108

## Measured regression coefficients
- Topical score (specific_topic): −0.293
- Keyword domain power: −0.186
- Crawl-frequency effects: strong positive linear relationships with impressions and traffic

## Product impact
The study's findings were used to refine the LLM Visibility tool (not SCHOLAR, despite appearing under a SCHOLAR section). The engineering team integrated these signals into relevance scoring, entity weighting, and crawl-sensitivity models. The system now prioritizes clarity, topical specificity, entity richness, and crawl frequency as dominant ranking predictors.
