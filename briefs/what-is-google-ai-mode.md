# Brief: What is Google AI Mode

### 1. Definition-first angle
Google AI Mode is a dedicated AI search experience inside Google Search, powered by a custom version of Gemini 3, that uses query fan-out and multi-step reasoning to answer complex prompts with cited web links, follow-up turns, and multimodal input.

### 2. Search intent breakdown
The user is trying to figure out what AI Mode actually is, how it differs from AI Overviews and ChatGPT, and what changes for them practically — either as a searcher (how to access it, what it can do) or as a marketer/SEO (how it affects clicks and citations). Most searchers want a clear definition, a clean comparison against AI Overviews, and proof it is not just a rebadged chatbot.

### 3. Content angle
Top-ranking pages (Zapier, Conductor, Searchable, Evergreen Media, NN/g) all open with a definition, explain query fan-out, and contrast AI Mode with AI Overviews. The shared gap across the SERP: most pages cite a generic "Gemini 2.5/3" without anchoring the model to a date, conflate the May 2025 "personal context" announcement with the January 2026 "Personal Intelligence" launch, and recycle CTR figures that measured AI Overviews while attributing them to AI Mode. This article will fix all three: anchor model versions to dates, separate Personal Context from Personal Intelligence, and only cite AI-Mode-specific data (Ahrefs Dec 2025 study) where relevant. It will also cover Deep Search, Search Live, and agentic booking with named partners — areas underdeveloped across the SERP.

### 8. Risks to avoid
- Calling AI Mode a "chatbot" without distinguishing it from ChatGPT (web-grounded, citation-heavy, search-tab integrated).
- Citing Pew's July 2025 CTR data as AI Mode evidence — Pew measured AI Overviews.
- Repeating the "93% zero-click" figure as fact — it traces to a vendor blog.
- Claiming Gemini 3 powered it at launch — launch was Gemini 2.0 (custom), then 2.5, then 3.
- Conflating "personal context" (May 2025 announcement) with "Personal Intelligence" (Jan 2026 launch).
- Treating AI Mode as US-only — it shipped to 180+ countries by August 2025.
- Treating it as an experiment — Labs requirement was removed in May 2025.
